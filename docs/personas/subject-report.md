## The differentiators (these are the whole positioning)

- **Analyst-graded — not AI.** This is the anti-competitor. Everyone else automates. Subject Report puts a human evaluator on every athlete.
- **Transcript ready in 48 hours.** Same 48-hour ops promise as Subject Medias.
- **Clip-linked to every grade.** Every score points to the exact film moment. Trust through receipts.
- **100% Film-backed.** No projections. No fabricated ceilings. What we saw, what we scored.
- **1:1 Placement.** Not a scores-and-goodbye platform. Human specialist attached.

---

## Voice signatures

- **Direct. No-nonsense. Anti-fluff.** The README ships in that voice: *"No framework. No build step. No dependencies."*
- **Contrarian framing.** *"Not hype. Hardware."* Positions against the whole recruiting-tech category.
- **Anti-AI-slop.** *"No AI scores"* is a POSITIONING claim, not a technical claim. The industry has flooded with synthetic evaluations. Subject Report's differentiator is that a human graded it.
- **Numbers matter.** 48 hours. 100%. $249 / $1,500 / $5,000 / $99/mo. Prices name the tier — no "let's chat about pricing" evasion.
- **Coaches, not creators.** The audience of the athlete's audience is college staff — write for how *they* read prospects, not for the athlete's parents' hearts.

## What NOT to write

- No *"AI-powered"* language — the anti-position IS "no AI scores"
- No *"unlock your potential"* motivational speaker filler
- No prices behind "contact us for pricing" — tier prices are public
- No hedging on the 48-hour turnaround claim
- No language that sounds like a subscription trap ("just $99/mo!")
- No talk of company vision on landing — the athlete is the subject, always
