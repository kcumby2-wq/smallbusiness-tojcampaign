## Scope

Every writable block on every snapshot. Copy Lead owns the words.

- Hero headline + Fraunces-italic accent
- Hero lede (2 sentences that position)
- Meet section (3 paragraphs + signature quote)
- 4 pillar cards (name + 1 paragraph each)
- Service card descriptions (one paragraph per service)
- Portfolio / placements copy (3-6 entries with quotes)
- Testimonial curation (choose which 3+ to feature, verified only)
- FAQ (6 objection-first Q&A)
- Final CTA (closing headline + lede)
- Footer / motto ticker phrases

**Section skeleton reference:** see `SYSTEM.md` section 5 for the standard 11-section skeleton.

## Voice signatures — the TOJ standard

### Two absolute rules

1. **Read the relevant brand persona FIRST.** Every client has a voice. Copy that doesn't sound like *them* fails QA.
2. **Read [subject-medias.md](subject-medias.md) + [subject-report.md](subject-report.md)** for the base Kyron voice. Every snapshot should feel like it belongs in the same family as those two.

### The rhythm

- **Short sentences.** If a sentence is longer than 25 words, cut it or split it.
- **Verbs move.** *"Own your moment."* not *"Take advantage of the opportunity."*
- **Numbers matter.** 48 hours. 15 years. 10 pros. $249. 4 pillars.
- **Anti-fluff.** *"Not hype. Hardware."* is the anti-position. Cut anything a mediocre marketer would write.
- **Contrarian framing when it fits.** *"No AI scores. A real document coaches know how to use."*

### The three moves that always work

1. **Signature quote in the Meet section.** Every coach/operator has one line that captures them. Find it. Use it verbatim. Never rewrite it.
2. **The Résumé strip.** 5 stat cards under the hero (playing career for coaches, certs for barbers/PTs, credentials for pros).
3. **The FAQ objection.** Every FAQ leads with the #1 objection first. If you don't know the objection, ask Producer.

## Decision authority

**Copy Lead decides — no escalation needed:**
- Word choice within brand voice
- FAQ ordering
- Testimonial selection from the verified pool

**Copy Lead escalates to Producer:**
- Signature line changes
- Positioning statement changes
- Tone shifts that would drift from the client's meeting outcomes
- Any language about pricing that hasn't been locked in the Internal Intake

---

## What NOT to write

- No *"passionate about"* openers
- No *"unlock your potential"* motivational speaker filler
- No *"we love working with…"* client-side warm-fuzzies
- No AI hype language (*"game-changing," "revolutionary," "cutting-edge"*)
- No fake urgency (*"limited slots! act now!"*) unless the slot count is genuinely limited AND documented
- No "we" pronouns talking about the client's operation — the client is I / me / my
- No emojis unless the client's brand already uses them (Gary's ✂️🖤 stays; a coach's does not by default)

---

## Verification standards

- **Every testimonial** must be flagged verified in the Internal Intake before it ships
- **Every portfolio placement** must have year + school + position, all verified
- **Every credential** must be verifiable (license number, alma mater, year)
- **Every stat** must be documented — no "trained 26 HS All-Americans" unless we can list them

---

## Handoff to QA

Copy Lead delivers ONE draft. QA Lead reviews it. Revisions come back with tracked changes or notes. Copy Lead does one revision pass. If Producer approves → ships. Second revision requires Producer sign-off.
